iVari Security Systems Pvt Ltd - Company Policies

Company policies are the guidelines and rules set by an organization to ensure consistent and fair behavior among employees and to establish the company's expectations. They cover a wide range of topics, from employee conduct to safety procedures. Here are some common types of company policies:

Code of Conduct:
Outlines expected behavior, including professionalism, ethics, and compliance with laws and regulations.
Attendance and Punctuality:
Details expectations for work hours, time off, and procedures for requesting leave.
Anti-Discrimination and Harassment:
Establishes a zero-tolerance stance towards discrimination and harassment and outlines procedures for reporting and handling complaints.
Health and Safety:
Covers protocols for maintaining a safe work environment, including emergency procedures, reporting hazards, and health regulations.
Confidentiality and Data Protection:
Specifies how to handle sensitive information and ensures compliance with data protection laws.
Dress Code:
Provides guidelines on acceptable attire in the workplace, which may vary based on the company's culture or the nature of the work.
Remote Work and Telecommuting:
Outlines the expectations and procedures for working from home or other remote locations.
Use of Company Property:
Defines acceptable use of company assets, including computers, vehicles, and other equipment.
Performance and Discipline:
Details the process for performance reviews, addressing performance issues, and disciplinary actions.
Social Media and Communication:

Expense Reimbursement:
Describes the process for claiming expenses incurred while performing job duties, including allowable expenses and documentation requirements.
Drug and Alcohol Policy:
Establishes rules regarding the use of drugs and alcohol in the workplace and any related testing procedures.
Training and Development:
Outlines opportunities for employee growth and the company's support for professional development.
Grievance and Complaint Procedures:
Provides a process for employees to raise concerns or complaints about work-related issues.
Termination and Resignation:
Details the procedures for ending employment, including notice periods and final pay.
